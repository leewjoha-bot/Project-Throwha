Throwha provides AI-powered coaching tools to help athletes improve. It is a supplement to — not a replacement for qualified human coaching, medical care, and professional sports science advice.

SECTION 01AI Coaching is a Tool, Not a Coach

The coaching feedback, technique analysis, training suggestions, and cues generated by the Throwha Platform are produced by artificial intelligence (powered by Anthropic's Claude). These outputs are designed to support your training — they are not a substitute for working with a qualified throws coach.

What AI Coaching Is

A supportive tool for pattern recognition, technique feedback, and training log analysis. Useful for supplementing professional guidance.

✗

What AI Coaching Is Not

A replacement for a qualified athletics coach, physiotherapist, sports doctor, or any credentialled professional.

SECTION 02Not Medical, Physiotherapy, or Health Advice

Nothing on the Throwha Platform constitutes medical advice, physiotherapy advice, sports medicine advice, or any form of health or clinical advice. Specifically, the Platform does not:

  • Diagnose or treat any injury, illness, condition, or disability;
  • Provide physiotherapy, rehabilitation, or recovery protocols;
  • Assess whether a training load or technique is safe for your individual body;
  • Advise on medications, supplements, or clinical interventions;
  • Replace a sports science, medical, or physio assessment.

SECTION 03Consult a Professional Before Changing Your Training

Before making any significant change to your training program, technique, load, or recovery based on AI-generated feedback from the Platform, you should consult:

  • A qualified throws coach or athletics coach accredited by your national federation;
  • A physiotherapist or sports medicine practitioner, if the change involves physical movement patterns or load;
  • A medical doctor, if you have any existing injury, health condition, or medical concern;
  • A sports scientist or strength and conditioning specialist where appropriate.

This is especially important for para athletes or athletes with disability-related classifications, where individual biomechanical and health considerations may differ significantly from general advice.
