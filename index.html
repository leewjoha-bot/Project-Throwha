01 — The case

Good coaching shouldn't be a privilege.

Whether you're in rural Queensland with no local club, a para athlete with no classification-specific coach, a self-coached competitor looking for an edge, or a beginner who just picked up a shot put for the first time — quality throws coaching should be available to you.

I'm fortunate to train under a Paralympian coach at the AIS. Most athletes aren't. Project Throwha exists so that every thrower — regardless of where they live, what they can afford, or what their body looks like — has access to expert coaching. No athlete should have to figure this out alone.

Lee O'Halloran — Australian & Oceania Record Holder, F46 Shot Put
// WHY IS IT FREE?

Project Throwha is funded by sponsors who believe good coaching shouldn't depend on your postcode, your income, or your disability. Athletes never pay. Sponsors fund the coaching infrastructure in exchange for the opportunity to support athletes on their journey — and to be part of the story when they succeed.

02 — Accessibility

Built accessible from day one.

Every athlete deserves a coach. Project Throwha is designed to work with screen readers, voice-first devices, and assistive technology. Visually impaired athletes (F11–F13) can use audio-first coaching mode — just tell the coach at your first session.

03 — What it does

Your coach. At the track. At 6am.

Everything a good throws coach does — available on your phone, whenever you need it.

01 — VOICE

Session Coaching

Log your session via text after training. The coach analyses what happened, reinforces what worked, and flags 1–2 things to fix. Voice notes coming soon.

02 — DIAG

Technique Diagnosis

Describe what you feel in the throw and get a specific diagnosis — not generic advice. The coach traces faults to root cause.

03 — BRIEF

Pre-Competition Brief

Competition morning: a brief built from your actual cue bank. Top 3 cues, mindset note, warm-up. Personalised to you.

04 — VIDEO

Video Analysis

Upload training footage straight to your coach. Exact camera angles, distances, and frame rates per event. Get a phase-by-phase review of your throw.

05 — WARMUP

Competition Warm-Up

A 12–15 minute warm-up built around your event and cues. No equipment. Four phases.

06 — LEARN

Gets Smarter Over Time

The more sessions you log, the better it knows you. A cue bank built around your body — not a generic athlete.

07 — S&C

Strength & Conditioning

Throws-specific S&C — mobility, power, injury prevention, recovery — built around rotational throwing demands.

08 — DRILLS

Drill Development

Break the throw into building blocks. Isolate each phase with targeted drills. Track progression.

07 — Who built this

Made by a thrower, for throwers.

Project Throwha was built by Lee O'Halloran — Australian & Oceania Record Holder in F46 Shot Put.

Lee spent years playing high-level able-bodied rugby in both Australia and Canada before picking up a shot put for the first time in December 2024. Within 12 months, he'd broken both the Australian and Oceania Records.

The technical knowledge he's built, been taught, has and hasn't had access to is the foundation of this project. Project Throwha is built for every throws athlete — able-bodied and para — from first session to competitive level.

// ON THE NAME

Three meanings in one word. In English, Throwha reads as Thrower. In te reo Māori — a nod to Lee's New Zealand Māori heritage — the wh carries an f sound, making it Throw-far. And hidden inside is O'Ha — the first syllable of his surname, O'Halloran — making the name literally part of who built it.

08 — For coaches

An on-demand coaching assistant.

Throwha doesn't replace coaches — it helps them maintain visibility across every athlete in their squad. Coaches get athlete-permissioned data, session insights, and squad-level tooling. Athletes are always free.

// THE TRUST MODEL — ATHLETE-CONTROLLED DATA SHARING

Coaches only see what athletes have explicitly shared with them. Athletes can revoke access at any time, and coaches cannot retain or copy data after revocation. The athlete-coach data flow is athlete-controlled at all times — by design.
